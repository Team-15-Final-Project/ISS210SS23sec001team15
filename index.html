About

Abortions are necessary in healthcare and with the recent overturn of Roe v. Wade, quality healthcare is being threatened. Abortions are needed for the physical and mental health of a pregnant person and everyone should have access to safe abortions. This overturn also disproportionately affects several categories of people, for instance women of color, members of the military and minors. Abortions are already physically strenuous but now people will seek unsafe options if they don’t have access to safe abortions. Not only does this overturn affect physical health but also mental health, “Women with unwanted pregnancies had 1.42 greater odds (95% CI = 1.07, 1.88) of having a depressive episode than did women who had planned pregnancies”. Women should have the rights to their own bodies and decide what’s best for them and their mental/physical health. Women of color are feeling harsher effects from this overturn, women of color receive on average more abortions than white women, “The higher rate of abortion among women of colour is mainly due to a higher rate of unintended pregnancies.13 Women of colour are already also impacted by poverty and lack of proper healthcare. Many studies have found that women in states where abortion restrictions have already been implemented often have to travel across state lines to access abortion services.14 Women of colour receive worse healthcare partly because of large disparities in health insurance coverage”. Minors are also still humans and can still get pregnant so why shouldn’t they have the same rights that other humans do? The mature minor rule states that minors that are determined to be mature enough can have some power over their medical treatment, “In fact, less than one-fifth of the states (8) have a broad mature minor exception to the standard requirement of paren- tal consent. The remainder have no exception at all (34), have significantly narrower or conditioned versions (6), or permit minors of any age to consent to treatment in all or specific circum- stances”. It may seem like minors have options however they are very limited and only under certain circumstances.

Women of Color

Women of color are disproportionately affected by the overturning of Roe v. Wade. Women of color have higher abortion rates due to the fact that they have more unintended pregnancies. These unintended pregnancies could lead to their death considering women of color have high maternal mortality rates and that they receive worse healthcare, which is largely due to the fact that they have worse health care coverage than their white counterparts. Women of color have far less flexibility to travel to another state for an abortion because the jobs they hold are less likely to give time off. The overturning of “the law of the land” is going to severely hurt and impact women of color more so than white women.

The Implications of Unintended Pregnancies for Mental Health in Later Life

Click Here to Open PDF


Unplanned pregnancies are common to women around the world, but the mental state of those women is not always talked about. Women who have unwanted pregnancies have a higher risk for anxiety and depression as well as illicit drug use and are more likely to smoke. It is important to have resources such as abortion available to everyone if need be. When taking a further look into unplanned/unwanted pregnancies, the children conceived are more prone to have behavioral and psychological issues in their future. The National Library of Medicine states "The large majority of the research focused on unintended pregnancies has attempted to document the effects of pregnancy intention on the health and development of infants and children — with poorer comparative outcomes found among children resulting from unintended pregnancies." Abortion should be something accessible to all people and the mental health of individuals being pressured to continue on with an unplanned pregnancy should be talked about more often./p>
